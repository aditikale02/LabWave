EXPERIMENT NO. 10 - SOLAR CELL -IV CHARACTRISTICS

AIM - APPARATUS - PRIOR CONCEPTS

Aim: To study the I-V characteristics of the solar cells to calculate the fill factor and value of resistance for maximum value of workable power.

Apparatus: solar cell, light source, variable load (potentiometer), milli-voltmeter, micro-ammeter, luxmeter etc.

Prior Concepts: photovoltaic effect, PN junction diode, solar cell.

THEORY

The photovoltaic effect is the direct conversion of solar energy (sunlight) into electricity through the absorption of light in a semiconductor material, which creates electron-hole pairs. When sunlight hits the solar cell, photons transfer energy to electrons, allowing them to escape their atomic bonds and generate current . A solar cell typically consists of: 1. A semiconductor (usually silicon) that absorbs sunlight and generates electron-hole pairs. 2. A drift region or electric field (formed at the p-n junction) to separate the charges. 3. Front and back electrodes to collect and transmit the electric current. In a p-n junction, electrons from the n-side and holes from the p-side diffuse across the junction, creating an electric field. This field drives the free electrons through an external circuit, producing direct current (DC). The current continues to flow as long as the cell is exposed to sunlight. Such a cell is known as a photovoltaic (PV) cell or solar cell, and multiple cells can be combined into modules or arrays. These can also be connected to inverters to produce alternating current (AC) for practical use .

Voc (Open-circuit Voltage): The maximum voltage when the solar cell is not connected to any load.

Isc (Short-circuit Current): The maximum current when the terminals are shorted.

MPP(Maximum Power Point): The point where the cell delivers maximum power, calculated as \text{MPP} = V_{mp} \times I_{mp}.

Fill Factor (FF): Indicates the quality of the solar cell, calculated as \text{FF} = \frac{V_{oc} \times I_{sc}}{V_{mp} \times I_{mp}}, typically between 0.7 and 0.8.

Efficiency (η): The ratio of the maximum electrical power output to the solar energy input, usually around 10–12% depending on the cell type.
