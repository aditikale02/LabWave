FOUR PROBE METHOD-2

AIM

To determine the band gap of germanium by the four probe method.

APPARATUS

Probe arrangement, sample, oven (0-200°C), constant current generator, oven power supply, and digital panel meter (measuring voltage and current).

PRIOR CONCEPTS

Basics of semiconductors, resistivity, and energy band gap.

THEORY

Ohm’s Law:

If physical conditions remains unchanged , then potential difference across two ens is proportional to current flowing through it.

V = IR

Resistivity (ρ):

It is the intrinsic property of a material that determines how much it resists current flow. The formula for resistivity is:

ρ = (R × A) / L

Unit of resistivity is ohm-meter

FOUR PROBE METHOD

The Four Probe Method is a widely used technique for measuring the resistivity of semiconductors.

FOUR PROBE METHOD SETUP

The four-point probe setup consists of four equally spaced tungsten metal tips with finite radius. Each tip is supported by srpings on the end to minimize sample damage during probing. A high impedance current source is used to supply current through the outer two probes, a voltmeter measures the voltage across the inner two probes to determine the sample resistivity. Typical probe spacing is ~2mm.These inner probes draw no current due to the high input impedance voltmeter in the circuit. Thus unwanted voltage drop caused by the contact resistance between probes and the sample is eliminated from the potential measurements.

The thickness and resistivity of the material are computed using the following formulas:

ρ = ρ(0) / f(w/S)

ρ(0) = (V/I) × 2πS

f(w/S) = 2S/w

PROCEDURE

  1. Select the semiconductor material.
  2. Select the source current.
  3. Select the oven range.
  4. Set the temperature.
  5. Select the voltmeter range.
  6. Measure the voltage.
  7. Calculate resistivity using:
    • ρ = ρ(0) / f(w/S)
    • ρ(0) = (V/I) × 2πS
    • f(w/S) = 2S/w
  8. Plot resistivity vs. temperature.
  9. Calculate the energy band gap using:
    • Eg = 2 × KB × slope of the graph
    • where KB = 8.6 × 10⁻⁵ eV/K
